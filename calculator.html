How Fat Loss Actually Works

🔥 Fat Loss = Calorie Deficit

Your body stores fat as an energy reserve. When you consistently eat fewer calories than you burn (your TDEE), your body taps into stored fat for fuel. Every 7,700 calories of deficit burns roughly 1kg of fat. There's no shortcut — the deficit is the mechanism.

💪 Fat Gain = Calorie Surplus

When you eat more calories than your body needs, the excess energy gets converted to fat and stored. It doesn't matter if those calories come from "clean" food — a surplus is a surplus. Understanding this makes tracking calories the single most powerful lever you have.

⚖️ Maintenance = Eat at TDEE

Your TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is the number of calories you burn across everything — resting, moving, digesting food. Consistently eating at TDEE keeps your weight stable. This is your baseline. Go below it to lose, above it to gain.

📊 BMR — The Foundation

Your Basal Metabolic Rate is how many calories your body burns just by existing — breathing, circulation, organ function. It's the floor. Your TDEE builds on top of it with your activity level multiplied in.

🥩 Why Protein Matters

Protein preserves muscle mass during a deficit. Without enough protein, your body may break down muscle for energy alongside fat. Aim for 1.6–2g per kg of bodyweight. It also keeps you fuller for longer — a huge advantage when cutting calories.

⏱️ Rate of Loss

A 500 calorie/day deficit creates a weekly deficit of ~3,500 kcal, roughly 0.5kg of fat/week. Faster is not always better — too aggressive a deficit risks muscle loss, energy crashes, and rebound eating. Sustainable beats fast every time.
